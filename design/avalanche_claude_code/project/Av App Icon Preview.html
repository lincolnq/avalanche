01 / ICON

The icon, as drawn

Rose gradient · plum bubble · cream counter. A sophisticated three-color hierarchy that sidesteps every "chat app = blue/green" expectation without going icy or twee.

What's working

The rose-pink gradient feels grown-up and grassroots at once — definitely not a startup-blue chat app. Plum on rose is an unusual, intentional pairing; reads as crafted.

You added a proper counter to the bubble for the icon (the wordmark keeps its solid form) — smart, gives the icon enough internal detail to be legible at small sizes.

Pairs well with

Warm earth-tone wallpapers, terracotta/rust UI accents, and a slightly darker wordmark (your existing #77260C burgundy works).

04 / LOCKUP

Icon + wordmark together

Two of your three brand objects in the same frame. Two different bubble shapes (icon has a counter, wordmark doesn't) — read carefully whether that feels intentional or inconsistent.

05 / NOTES

What I'd push on

Three reactions, one to keep and two to consider.

+ KEEP

The three-color hierarchy is rare and right

Background-gradient → solid bubble → cream counter sets up a clear z-order. Most chat icons are 1–2 color and feel flat. This reads as deliberate brand-craft.

⚠ FLAG

2-px cream stroke around the bubble disappears at small sizes

At 60px and below the outer stroke vanishes into the gradient and the bubble starts to feel like a sticker. Consider thickening to 3px, or removing it entirely and relying on color contrast alone (it's enough).

⚠ FLAG

Two different 'a' shapes

Wordmark 'a' has no counter; icon 'a' does. Reads as intentional once you notice, but it's a subtle inconsistency. Options: (a) keep both, treat as wordmark-vs-mark distinction; (b) add the counter to the wordmark 'a' too; (c) drop the counter from the icon and rely on tail alone. I lean (a) — but worth deciding on purpose.

→ IDEA

Counter color = brand accent

The cream counter is currently a "neutral" — what if it became the brand's named accent color? Suddenly your accent has a home in the logo itself, not just in UI.

→ IDEA

Try the gradient direction

Current gradient is light-top → dark-bottom (gravity / settling). Inverting it (dark-top → light-bottom) would feel more like an emanation / signal. Worth a test.

→ IDEA

Plum gets its own role

You've added plum to the palette without it appearing in the wordmark. Worth promoting to a core brand color — works beautifully as dark mode surface, secondary buttons, focused link states.
